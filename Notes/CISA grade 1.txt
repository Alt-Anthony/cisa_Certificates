INDUSTRIAL CONTROL SYSTEM
what is an ics?
facilities, systems and equipment that comprise the 
operational real time control enviroment or services
 interconnection of components  
types
SCADA: system in gas , oil , water and electricity
DCS: power generation, chemical processing oil refining
and wastewater treatment
PCS: water treatment chemical processing, mining,
pharmaceutical, manufacturing
EMS: system of computer aided tools used by operators
electrical energy and pump optimization
AS: SYSTEM WITHOUT HUMAN INTERVENTION, manufacturing
SIS: used on critical proccess safety systems,
Other: BAS: puertas automaticas or controles de ventilacion
or air conditioning
Cybersecurity
anteriormente no era necesario la cyberseguridad por 
que las ics no estaban conectados, estas
vulnerabilidades son dificiles de controlar en un entorno
estas fueron introducidas cuando los propietarios buscaron
productos con mejores funcionalidades en la tecnologia
de la informacion(IT)como acceso remoto e interconectividad

TYPES OF FACILITIES
Site Facilities: Produce products
Transmission Facilities: Carry electricity oil or gas and water
Generation Facilities: Produce electrical energy
Distribution Facilities: Distribute products to the costumers

DISCRETE MANUFACTURING
Involves products you can touch and is not continuous, it can
be stopped or started at any time, assembly robots are 
used in discrete manufacturing
PROCESS MANUFACTURING
Involves products made by using formulas or recipes, once
the product is complete it can't be taken apart to get
the original components. There are 3 types:

Continous: it requires an uninterrupted flow of raw
material from start to finish, run constantly unless
